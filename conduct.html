Reflection on Professional Conduct

This page covers professional behaviour, ethics, and lessons learned during academic and personal development relevant to future workplace expectations.

What we have covered in class, especially the BCS Code of Conduct, has greatly influenced how I view professional conduct. I now recognise the value of honesty, responsibility, and decency toward others in the workplace, particularly when considering the potential effects of our actions on clients, coworkers, and the general public.

I've begun to think more carefully about how these ideas apply to my behaviour, even though the majority of my awareness stems from scholarly discussions rather than practical experience. For instance, I was aware that I still had to make up lost time when I was unable to attend a portion of the System Analysis and Design course because of health concerns. I took the initiative to study the material on my own, not only to complete the course requirements but also because I believed it was important to practice personal accountability, which is something the Code emphasises a lot.

In a similar vein, I strive to respect diverse opinions and make a positive contribution in group situations and workshops, keeping in mind that professionalism influences team trust and success. Nevertheless, I am aware that there is still a disconnect between my theoretical understanding of professionalism and my consistent application of it in the workplace. As I continue to grow, I'm trying to close that gap.
